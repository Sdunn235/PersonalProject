# World Simulation Design

## Core Philosophy

The world is not a stage.

It is a **system of interacting processes**.

---

## Persistence Rules

### Terrain
- Can be modified over time
- Resource extraction depletes areas
- Regrowth occurs naturally

---

### Settlements
- Form based on resource availability
- Grow through population and trade
- Decline if resources fail

---

### Resources
- Finite but renewable depending on type
- Distributed unevenly
- Drive movement and conflict

---

## Economy Model

No fixed economy.

Emerges from:
- Resource scarcity
- Supply/demand
- NPC production and consumption

---

## Factions

Not fully finalized.

Expected features:
- Shared identity
- Territory
- Alliances/conflicts

---

## Living World Criteria

A system qualifies as "living" if:
- It changes without player input
- It produces new states over time
- It cannot be fully predicted

---

## Time Scale

- Short-term: moment-to-moment decisions
- Mid-term: settlement growth
- Long-term: environmental change

---

## Non-Negotiable Rule

If the world resets or depends entirely on player interaction,
it is not a simulation.
